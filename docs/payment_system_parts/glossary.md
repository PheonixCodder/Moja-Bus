# Glossary

[⬅️ Back to README](./README.md)

---

This glossary defines the strict domain language used across the Moja Ride financial codebase. When discussing the system, these terms must be used exactly as defined here to avoid catastrophic misunderstandings.

## Accounting Terms

- **Double-Entry Ledger**: An accounting system where every transaction requires at least one debit and one credit, and the total debits must exactly equal the total credits.
- **Asset**: A resource owned by the platform that holds positive economic value (e.g., Cash in Paystack). Normal balance is Debit.
- **Liability**: An obligation the platform owes to an external party (e.g., Funds owed to an Operator). Normal balance is Credit.
- **Revenue**: Income generated by the platform (e.g., Commissions). Normal balance is Credit.
- **Expense**: Costs incurred by the platform (e.g., Processor Fees). Normal balance is Debit.
- **Debit (DR)**: An entry on the left side of a ledger. Increases Assets/Expenses, decreases Liabilities/Revenue.
- **Credit (CR)**: An entry on the right side of a ledger. Increases Liabilities/Revenue, decreases Assets/Expenses.
- **Posted Balance**: The absolute mathematical sum of all ledger entries for an account.
- **Reserved Balance**: A portion of the posted balance that is temporarily locked in escrow and cannot be withdrawn.
- **Available Balance**: The portion of the posted balance that is free to be utilized (`Posted - Reserved`).

## Domain Terms

- **Basis Points (Bps)**: A unit of measure used for percentages to avoid floating-point math. 100 Bps = 1%. 500 Bps = 5%.
- **Idempotency**: The property of an operation that guarantees running it multiple times yields the exact same result as running it once.
- **Orphaned Payment**: A scenario where a passenger successfully pays on Paystack, but their cart (`HoldGroup`) expired before the platform could confirm it.
- **Clawback**: Reversing a previously earned commission due to a refund or cancellation.
- **Escrow**: A state where funds are held by the platform in a liability account's `reservedBalance` until a service is fully rendered.

## Technical Terms

- **Pessimistic Locking**: A database strategy (`SELECT FOR UPDATE`) that physically prevents other transactions from reading or writing a row until the lock is released.
- **Deadlock**: A fatal database state where Transaction A waits for Transaction B, and Transaction B waits for Transaction A, causing both to hang infinitely.
- **Webhook Storm**: A phenomenon where an external gateway delivers the same event payload dozens or hundreds of times simultaneously.
